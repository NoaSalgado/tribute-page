Janis Joplin

The first female rock star

Timeline

1943: Born on Jan. 19 in Port Arthur, Texas

1950s:Was a good student and sang in a church choir, but she was misfit and a rebel. She began listening to blues and jazz.

After high school, she tool secretarial courses and worked in a local library and also waitressed.

1962: She was studying art at the University of Texas and she played gigs at informal venues on campus and at the Threadgill´s bar

1963: Left school and headed for San Francisco, in hopes of getting her muscial career off the ground.She performed on a side stage at the 1963 Monterrey Folk Festival.
Living in the Bay Area, her drug use increased as did her drinking.

1966-1967: Auditioned and joined to the Big Brother band in San Francisco. The band grew popular and performed at the Monterey Pop Festival in 1967.

1968: Big Brother signed with Columbia Records, and Albert Grossman (Bob Dylan), became their manager. Their first album "Chip`Thirlls" went gold, but the attention paid to Joplin´s talents caused tension among band members.

1969: Janis released her album "I Got Dem Ol`Kozmic Blues Again Mama! in September, whith Kozmic Blues Band.

Joplin grew addicted to heroin. She tried to kick the habit several times.

1970: In Los Angeles, Joplin was recording the album “Pearl” with the Full Tilt Boogie Band in 1970. She wrote the album’s cut “Move Over” and co-wrote the a cappella ditty “Mercedes Benz” with a beat poet named Michael McClure.

The singer, then 27 years old, accidentally overdosed on Oct. 4, 1970, in a room at Hollywood's Landmark Hotel. After she was cremated, her ashes were scattered in the Pacific Ocean and along Stinson Beach in Northern California.
